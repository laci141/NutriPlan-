Visibility Gap — Under-cited Authors
local DB · gap score
DEI Officers · Journal Editors · Promotion Committees

Identify Lancet authors with high output but unexpectedly low citations — the “visibility gap.” A positive gap score means the author publishes well but is cited less than expected given their publication volume. Surfaces systematic under-recognition.

Q14 · Visibility gap at Oxford
./thelancet visibility-gap --institution "Oxford"
Q15 · Visibility gap at MSK
./thelancet visibility-gap --institution "MSK"

Methodology: Gap score = (expected citations based on work count) − (actual citations), normalized 0–1. Score of +0.95 means the author receives only ~5% of their “expected” citation weight — a strong signal of under-recognition or topic bias in the citation network.
When to use
A journal editor wants to audit whether certain research groups are systematically under-cited in their journal’s reference network. visibility-gap --institution "Oxford" shows 4 Oxford authors at gap +0.95 — a pattern suggesting a structural bias worth investigating, not just individual variation.

Refresh — Sync Articles to Local DB
live API → SQLite
All Users · Initial Setup · Regular Maintenance

Populate the local SQLite database with recent Lancet articles from OpenAlex. Required before running any analytics command (rank-authors, mesh, affiliation-growth, drift, curate, visibility-gap). Set --max-pages to control how many articles are fetched.

Q18 · Sync The Lancet (1,000 articles)
./thelancet refresh --journal lancet --max-pages 10
Q19 · Sync Lancet Oncology (600 articles)
./thelancet refresh --journal lancet-oncology --max-pages 6
Q20 · Sync multiple journals in a loop
for j in lancet lancet-oncology lancet-neurology; do ./thelancet refresh --journal $j --max-pages 5; done
